# 📘 Chapter 8.2: Regularization Techniques

---

## 🧠 Why This Chapter Matters

After tuning hyperparameters, you may notice:

> ✅ Model performs well on training data
> ❌ But performs poorly on new (unseen) data

This problem is called:

> ⚠️ **Overfitting**

---

## 🔑 What is Regularization?

> Regularization is a set of techniques used to **prevent overfitting and improve generalization**.

👉 In simple words:

* Make model **learn patterns**, not **memorize data**

---

## 🧩 Simple Analogy

Think of a student:

* Memorizes answers → fails in new questions ❌
* Understands concepts → performs well everywhere ✅

👉 Regularization makes the model like the second student

---

## 🔍 What is Overfitting?

* Model learns **training data too perfectly**
* Fails on new data

# 🔧 Common Regularization Techniques in RNN

---

## 1️⃣ Dropout (Most Important)

---

### 🧠 What it does:

> Randomly turns off some neurons during training

---

### ⚙️ Example

```python id="reg1"
self.rnn = nn.RNN(input_size=1, hidden_size=10, dropout=0.3)
```

---

### 🧠 Why it helps

* Prevents model from depending too much on specific neurons
* Forces it to learn **robust patterns**

---

### 🧩 Analogy

Group study:

* Some students absent
* Others must learn independently

👉 Leads to better understanding

---

## 2️⃣ L2 Regularization (Weight Decay)

---

### 🧠 What it does:

> Penalizes large weights

---

### ⚙️ Example

```python id="reg2"
optimizer = torch.optim.Adam(model.parameters(), lr=0.01, weight_decay=0.001)
```

---

### 🧠 Why it helps

* Keeps weights small
* Prevents extreme behavior

---

### 🧩 Analogy

Like controlling expenses:

* Avoid unnecessary extremes

---

## 3️⃣ Early Stopping

---

### 🧠 What it does:

> Stop training when performance stops improving

---

### 🧠 Why it helps

* Prevents over-learning
* Saves time

---

### 🧩 Analogy

Studying:

* Stop when you understand
* Don’t over-study and confuse yourself

---

## 4️⃣ Data Augmentation (Limited in RNN)

---

### 🧠 Idea:

* Increase data variety

---

### Example:

* Slight variations in time-series
* Synonym replacement in text

---

👉 More data → better generalization

---

## 5️⃣ Gradient Clipping

---

### 🧠 What it does:

> Limits gradient values during training

---

### ⚙️ Example

```python id="reg3"
torch.nn.utils.clip_grad_norm_(model.parameters(), max_norm=1.0)
```

---

### 🧠 Why it helps

* Prevents exploding gradient
* Stabilizes training

---

## ⚖️ Summary of Techniques

| Technique         | Purpose             |
| ----------------- | ------------------- |
| Dropout           | Reduce dependency   |
| L2 Regularization | Control weight size |
| Early Stopping    | Avoid over-training |
| Data Augmentation | Increase diversity  |
| Gradient Clipping | Stabilize training  |

---

## 🧠 Best Practical Strategy

Start with:

* Dropout = 0.2–0.5
* Weight decay = small value
* Monitor validation loss

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Too much dropout

👉 Model cannot learn properly

---

### ❌ Ignoring validation data

👉 Cannot detect overfitting

---

### ❌ Training too long

👉 Leads to memorization

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> Regularization helps the model focus on **general patterns**, not noise.

---

## 📌 One-Line Summary

> Regularization techniques prevent overfitting by controlling model complexity and improving generalization.

---

## 🎯 What You Should Be Able to Do

After this chapter:

* Identify overfitting
* Apply dropout and weight decay
